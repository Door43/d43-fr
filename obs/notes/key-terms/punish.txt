====== punish : punir  F ======

==== (to punish, punishment) : punir, punition ====

===== Definition: Définition =====

To punish someone is to cause that person to suffer a negative consequence for doing something wrong.

Punir une personne, c'est forcer cette personne à endurer la conséquences négative d'une mauvaise action.

  * God punished the Israelites when they disobeyed him, especially when they worshiped false gods.
  * Dieu a puni les Israélites quand ils Lui avaient désobéi, surtout lorsque ils ont adoré de faux dieux.
  * Sometimes punishment is intended to motivate a person to stop sinning, as when God punished the Israelites.
  * Parfois l'intention derrière une punition est de motiver une personne à s'éloigner du péché, comme quand Dieu a puni les Israélites.
  * Jesus was punished for all the evil things that every person has ever done. He received each person's punishment on himself even though he did nothing wrong and did not deserve that punishment.
  * Jésus a été puni pour tout le mal commis par tout le monde. Il a porté sur lui le chatiment de chaque personne alors qu'Il n'avait rien fait de mal et ne méritait pas cette punition.

===== Examples from the Bible stories: =====

  * **[[:fr:obs:notes:frames:13-07|[13-07]]]** God also gave many other laws and rules to follow. If the people obeyed these laws, God promised that he would bless and protect them. If they disobeyed them, God would **punish**  them.
  * **[[:fr:obs:notes:frames:16-02|[16-02]]]** Because the Israelites kept disobeying God, he **punished**  them by allowing their enemies to defeat them.
  * **[[:fr:obs:notes:frames:19-16|[19-16]]]** The prophets warned the people that if they did not stop doing evil and start obeying God, then God would judge them as guilty, and he would **punish**  them.
  * **[[:fr:obs:notes:frames:48-06|[48-06]]]** Jesus was the perfect high priest because he took the **punishment**  for every sin that anyone has ever committed.
  * **[[:fr:obs:notes:frames:48-10|[48-10]]]** When anyone believes in Jesus, the blood of Jesus takes away that person's sin, and God's **punishment**  passes over him.
  * **[[:fr:obs:notes:frames:49-09|[49-09]]]** But God loved everyone in the world so much that he gave his only Son so that whoever believes in Jesus will not be **punished**  for his sins, but will live with God forever.
  * **[[:fr:obs:notes:frames:49-11|[49-11]]]** Jesus never sinned, but he chose to be **punished**  and die as the perfect sacrifice to take away your sins and the sins of every person in the world.
