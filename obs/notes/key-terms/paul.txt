====== Paul: Paul ======

==== (Paul, Saul) (Paul, Saul) ====

===== Facts: Les faits =====

Paul was a leader of the early church who was sent by Jesus to take the good news to many other people groups.

Paul était un dirigeant des premières églises, qui a été envoyé par Jésus pour apporter la bonne nouvelle aux autres peuples

  * Paul was a Jew who was born in the Roman city of Tarsus, and was therefore also a Roman citizen.
  * Paul était un Juif, né en dans la ville romaine de Tarse, et cependant avait également la citoyenneté romaine.
  * Paul was originally called by his Jewish name, Saul.
  * <font 14.166666030883789px line-height: 15.833333015441895px;/inherit;;inherit;;inherit>A</font> <font 14.166666030883789px line-height: 15.833333015441895px;/inherit;;inherit;;inherit></font> l'origine, Paul était appelé par son nom Juif, Saul.
  * Saul became a Jewish religious leader and arrested Jews who became Christians because he did not believe in Jesus.
  * Saul est devenu un dirigeant juif religieux et arretait les Juifs qui devenaient des chrétiens parce qu'ils n:ont cru en Jesus
  * Jesus came to Saul in a blinding light and told him to stop hurting Christians. Jesus then sent a Christian man to teach Saul, and Saul believed in Jesus.
  * Jesus venait vers Saul dans une lumiere ebloussante et lui disai td"arreter de pourchasser les Chretiens. Jesus envoya alors un chretien enseignait Saul et Saul acceptait Jesus
  * At first, Saul taught Jews about Jesus.
  * D'abord Saul enseignait les Juifs au sujet de Jesus
  * Later, God sent Saul to teach other people groups about Jesus in many Roman cities. Then he started using the Roman name, Paul.
  * Ensuite, Dieu envoyait Saul enseignait les groupes des gens au sujet de Jesus dans certaines villes romaines
  * Paul also wrote letters to encourage and teach Christians in various cities. Some of his letters are in the Bible. PAUL aussi a ecrit des lettres pour encourager et enseigner les Christiens dans diverses villes

===== Examples from the Bible stories: =====

  * **[[:fr:obs:notes:frames:45-06|[45-06]]]** A young man named **Saul**  agreed with the people who killed Stephen and guarded their robes while they threw stones at him.
  * **[[:fr:obs:notes:frames:46-01|[46-01]]]****Saul**  was the young man who guarded the robes of the men who killed Stephen. He did not believe in Jesus, so he persecuted the believers.
  * **[[:fr:obs:notes:frames:46-02|[46-02]]]** While **Saul**  was on his way to Damascus, a bright light from heaven shone all around him, and he fell to the ground. **Saul**  heard someone say, "**Saul**! **Saul**! Why do you persecute me?"
  * **[[:fr:obs:notes:frames:46-05|[46-05]]]** So Ananias went to **Saul**, placed his hands on him, and said, "Jesus who appeared to you on your way here, sent me to you so that you can regain your sight and be filled with the Holy Spirit." **Saul**  immediately was able to see again, and Ananias baptized him.
  * **[[:fr:obs:notes:frames:46-06|[46-06]]]** Right away, **Saul**  began preaching to the Jews in Damascus, saying, "Jesus is the Son of God!"
  * **[[:fr:obs:notes:frames:46-09|[46-09]]]** Barnabas and **Saul**  went there (Antioch) to teach these new believers more about Jesus and to strengthen the church.
  * **[[:fr:obs:notes:frames:47-01|[47-01]]]** As **Saul**  traveled throughout the Roman Empire, he began to use his Roman name, "**Paul**."
  * **[[:fr:obs:notes:frames:47-14|[47-14]]]****Paul**  and other Christian leaders traveled to many cities, preaching and teaching people the good news about Jesus.
