====== mock : SE MOQUER DE ======

===== Definition: =====

To deride or make fun of someone, especially in a cruel way.

**Tourner en derisionou rendre grottesque quelqu'un,especialement en cruelle maniere**

  * Mocking often involves taunting someone and laughing at them.
  * **La moquerie implique souvent des insultes envers quelqu'un et des railleries en son endroit**
  * To mock someone is to pretend to honor them, but in a way that actually shows contempt for them.
  * Se **moquer de quelqu'un c'est pretendre l'honorer**
  * A person can also mock someone by imitating that person's words or actions with the intent to embarrass them.
  * une personne peut aussi se moquer de quelqu'un en imitant ses mots ou ses actions  en tentant de l'embarrasser

===== Examples from the Bible stories: =====

  * **[[:fr:obs:notes:frames:21-12|[21-12]]]** Isaiah prophesied that people would spit on, **mock**, and beat the Messiah.
  * **[[:fr:obs:notes:frames:39-05|[39-05]]]** The Jewish leaders all answered the high priest, "He deserves to die!" Then they blindfolded Jesus, spit on him, hit him, and **mocked**  him.
  * **[[:fr:obs:notes:frames:39-12|[39-12]]]** The soldiers whipped Jesus, and put a royal robe and a crown made of thorns on him. Then they **mocked**  him by saying, "Look, the King of the Jews!"
  * **[[:fr:obs:notes:frames:40-04|[40-04]]]** Jesus was crucified between two robbers. One of them **mocked**  Jesus, but the other said, "Do you have no fear of God?"
  * **[[:fr:obs:notes:frames:40-05|[40-05]]]** The Jewish leaders and the other people in the crowd **mocked**  Jesus. They said to him, "If you are the Son of God, come down from the cross and save yourself! Then we will believe you."
