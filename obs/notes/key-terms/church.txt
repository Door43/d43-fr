====== church  église  F ======

==== (church, Church) ====

(église, Eglise)

===== Definition: Définition =====

The word, "church" refers to a group of people who believe in Jesus. When those believers are in a local area such as a city or a region, it is written as, "church." When it refers to believers everywhere, it is written as, "Church."

Le mot "église"se réfère à un groupe de gens qui croit en Jésus. Quand ces croyants sont dans une localité comme une ville où une région, on peut utiliser le mot "église". Quand le mot se réfère à des croyants partout, on l'écrit "Eglise" .

  * The original meaning of the word for "church" in the Bible is "called out (to meet together)."
  * Le sens original du mot "église'dans la Bible est "appelé à (hors de, en se rassemblant )
  * "Church" could be translated as a "gathering together" or "assembly" of believers. The word "congregation" has a similar meaning.
  * "l'Eglise"pourrait être traduite comme "rassemblement de tout le monde" ou "l'assemblée" des croyants. Le mot "congrégation" a un sens similaire.
  * However, the believers are still the "church" even when they are not together.
  * Cependant, les croyants demeurent "l'église" même quand ils ne sont pas ensemble.
  * In the Bible, the word "church" always refers to Christian believers, and never to a building.
  * Dans la Bible, le mot "église" se refère toujours aux croyants chrétiens, mais jamais au bâtiment.
  * The church meets together to pray, teach God's truth, and encourage each other to live in a way that is pleasing to God.
  * L'église se rasemble pour prier, enseigner la vérité de Dieu et encourager tout un chacun pour vivre qui plaise à Dieu.
  * God also calls his people, the Church, to go out and teach the good message about Jesus.
  * Dieu a appelé aussi son peuple, l'Eglise, d'aller partout et enseigner la Bonne Nouvelle de Jésus.

  * **[[:fr:obs:notes:frames:43-12|[43-12]]]** About 3,000 people believed what Peter said and became disciples of Jesus. They were baptized and became part of the **church**  at Jerusalem.
  * **[[:fr:obs:notes:frames:46-09|[46-09]]]** Most of the people in Antioch were not Jews, but for the first time, very many of them also became believers. Barnabas and Saul went there to teach these new believers more about Jesus and to strengthen the **church**.
  * **[[:fr:obs:notes:frames:46-10|[46-10]]]** So the **church**  in Antioch prayed for Barnabas and Saul and placed their hands on them. Then they sent them off to preach the good news of Jesus in many other places.
  * **[[:fr:obs:notes:frames:47-13|[47-13]]]** The good news of Jesus kept spreading, and the **Church**  kept growing.
  * **[[:fr:obs:notes:frames:50-01|[50-01]]]** For almost 2,000 years, more and more people around the world have been hearing the good news about Jesus the Messiah. The **Church**  has been growing.
