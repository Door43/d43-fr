====== Babylon : Babylone ======

==== (Babylon, Babylonian): Babylone, Babylonien ====

===== Facts: Faits =====

The ancient city of Babylon was located along the Euphrates River. Babylon was the capital of the Babylonian Empire which was located in the region of what is now the country of Iraq.

**L'ancienne ville de Babylone etait installee le long de la river Euphrate. Babylone etait la capitale de l'empire babylonien qui etait localise dans la region de ce qu'est maintenant  l'Etat d'Iraq**

   * Babylon was located in the same region where the Tower of Babel had been built hundreds of years before.
  * **Babylone etait localise dans la meme  regionou la tour de Babel avait ete detruite des annees au paravant**
  *
  * The Babylonians were a powerful people group that attacked the kingdom of Judah and held some of them captive for 70 years.
  * **Les  babyloniens un groupe de peuple puisant qui attaquaient le Royaume de Juda et aidaient quelqu<un de3s esclave durant soixante dix ans**
  *
  * Sometimes the word "Babylon" is used to refer to the entire Babylonian Empire. The "king of Babylon" was king not only of that city, but of the whole empire.
  * **Parfois le mot Babylone eait utilise pour se referer a l'empire babylonien tout entier. (The Roi de Babylone)  n'etait pas seulement le roi de la ville mais roi de tout l'empire **

===== Examples from the Bible stories: =====

  * **[[:fr:obs:notes:frames:20-06|[20-06]]]** About 100 years after the Assyrians destroyed the kingdom of Israel, God sent Nebuchadnezzar, king of the **Babylonians**, to attack the kingdom of Judah. **Babylon**  was a powerful empire.
  * **[[:fr:obs:notes:frames:20-07|[20-07]]]** But after a few years, the king of Judah rebelled against **Babylon**. So, the **Babylonians**  came back and attacked the kingdom of Judah. They captured the city of Jerusalem, destroyed the Temple, and took away all the treasures of the city and the Temple.
  * **[[:fr:obs:notes:frames:20-09|[20-09]]]** Nebuchadnezzar and his army took almost all of the people of the kingdom of Judah to **Babylon**, leaving only the poorest people behind to plant the fields.
  * **[[:fr:obs:notes:frames:20-11|[20-11]]]** About seventy years later, Cyrus, the king of the Persians, defeated **Babylon**.
