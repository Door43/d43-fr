====== Pilate: Pilate ======

===== Facts: Les faits =====

Pilate was the Roman governor who sentenced Jesus to death.

**Pilate etait le gouverneur romain qui condamnait a mort Jesus**

   * Because Pilate was the governor, he had the authority to put criminals to death.
  * **Du fait que Pilate etait le governeur, ainsi il avait le pouvoir de mettre les criminels a mort**
  * The Jewish religious leaders wanted Pilate to crucify Jesus, so they lied and said that Jesus was a criminal**.**
  * **les dirigeants religieux  Juifs ont voulu que Pilate crucifie  Jesus aisnsi ils ont menti en pretestant que Jesus etait un criminel**
  * Pilate realized that Jesus was not guilty, but he was afraid of the crowd, so he ordered his soldiers to crucify Jesus.
  * **Pilate a realise que Jesus n'etait pas coupable.  mais il avait peur de la foule, aisni il  donna l'ordre a ses soldats de crucifier Jesus**

===== Examples from the Bible stories: =====

  * **[[:fr:obs:notes:frames:39-09|[39-09]]]** Early the next morning, the Jewish leaders brought Jesus to **Pilate**, the Roman governor. They hoped that **Pilate**  would condemn Jesus as guilty and sentenced him to be killed. **Pilate**  asked Jesus, "Are you the King of the Jews?"
  * **[[:fr:obs:notes:frames:39-10|[39-10]]]****Pilate**  said, "What is truth?"
  * **[[:fr:obs:notes:frames:39-11|[39-11]]]** After speaking with Jesus, **Pilate**  went out to the crowd and said, "I find no guilt in this man." But the Jewish leaders and the crowd shouted, "Crucify him!" **Pilate**  replied, "He is not guilty." But they shouted even louder. Then **Pilate**  said a third time, "He is not guilty!"
  * **[[:fr:obs:notes:frames:39-12|[39-12]]]****Pilate**  became afraid that the crowd would begin to riot, so he ordered his soldiers to crucify Jesus.
  * **[[:fr:obs:notes:frames:40-02|[40-02]]]****Pilate**  commanded that a sign be put above Jesus' head that read, "King of the Jews."
  * **[[:fr:obs:notes:frames:41-02|[41-02]]]****Pilate**  said, "Take some soldiers and make the tomb as secure as you can."
