====== judge, judgment(juger, jugement) ======

===== Definition: Définition: =====

The terms "judge" and "judgment" often refer to making a decision about whether something  is morally right or wrong.


Les termes "juger" et "jugement" signifient souvent décider si quelquechose est moralement bon ou mauvais.

  * The "judgment of God" often refers to his decision to condemn something or someone as sinful.
  * "Le jugement de Dieu" fait souvent référence à la décision de Dieu  de condamner quelquechose ou quelqu'un 
  * God's judgment usually includes punishing people for their sin.
  * le jugement de Dieu comprend normalement le fait de punir les gens pour leurs péchés.
  * The term "judge" can also mean "condemn." God instructs his people not to judge each other in this way.
  * Le terme "juger" peut aussi signifier "condamner." Dieu instruit son peuple de ne pas juger les uns les autres de cette manière.
  * Another meaning is "arbitrate between" or "judge between," as in deciding which person is right in a dispute between them.  * In some contexts, God's judgments are his decrees or judicial decisions. They are similar to his laws or his precepts.
  * 
  * "Judgment" can refer to wise decision-making ability. A person who lacks "judgment" does not have the wisdom to make wise decisions.
===== Translation Suggestions: Suggestions de traduction: =====

  * Depending on the context, other ways to translate "to judge" could include, "to decide" or "to condemn" or "to punish" or "to decree."
  * The term "judgment" could be translated as, "punishment" or "decision" or "verdict" or "decree" or "condemnation."
  * In some contexts, the phrase "in the judgment" could also be translated as "on judgment day" or "during the time when God judges people."
