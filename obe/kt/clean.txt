====== clean, cleanse/pure, purifier ======

====== les Faits: ======  

===== Definition: Définition =====

The tern "clean" literally means to not have any dirt or stain. In the Bible, it is often used figuratively to mean, "pure," "holy," or "free from sin."

Le terme "pure" signifie littéralement être sans saleté ou sans tache. il est souvent employé au sens figuré et veut dire "pure," "saint" ou "sans péché."

'"Cleanse" is the process of making something "clean." It could also be translated as "wash" or "purify."

"Purifier" est rendre quelquechose "pure". On pourrait aussi le traduire par "laver" ou "rendre pure."

  * In the Old Testament, God told the Israelites which animals were “clean” and which ones were “unclean.” Only the clean animals were permitted to be used for eating or for sacrifice.
  * Dans l'Ancien Testament, Dieu a dit aux Israélites quels animaux étaient "purs" et ceux qui étaient "impurs."  Les animaux purs seuls étaient permis pour la nourriture et le sacrifice.
  * A person who had certain skin diseases would be unclean until the skin was healed enough to no longer be contagious. Instructions for cleansing the skin had to be obeyed in order for that person to be declared "clean" again.
  * Une personne atteinte de certaines maladies de la peau était impure jusqu'à ce que la guérison de la peau soit établie pour éviter la contagion. Des instruction pour la purification de la peau devait être suivies afin que cette personne soit déclarée "pure" de nouveau.
  * Sometimes "clean" is used figuratively to refer to moral purity.
  * On utlise quelquefois le terme "pure" au sens figuré pour parler de la pureté morale.

===== Translation Suggestions: =====

  * This term could be translated with the common word for "clean" or "pure" (in the sense of being not dirty).
  * "Cleanse" could be translated by "wash" or "purify."
  * Make sure that the words used for "clean" and "cleanse" can also be understood in a figurative sense.
