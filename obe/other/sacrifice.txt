====== sacrifice, offering(sacrifice, offrande) ======

===== Definition: Définition: =====

In the Bible, the terms "sacrifice" and "offering" refer to special gifts given to God as an act of worshiping him. People also offered sacrifices to false gods.


Dans la bible les termes "sacrifice" et "offrande" font référence aux cadeaux spéciaux donnés à Dieu lorsqu'on l'adore. Des gens offraient aussi des sacrifices à de faux dieux.
 l'holocauste" et "sacrifice de paix" 

  * The word "offering" generally refers to anything that is offered or given. The term "sacrifice" refers to something that is given or done at great cost to the giver.
  * Le mot "offrande" fait généralement référence à ce que l'on offre ou donne. Le terme "sacrifice" fait référence à ce qui nous a coûté un grand prix en ce qui concerne notre don ou notre action.
  * Offerings to God were specific things that he commanded the Israelites to give in order to express devotion and obedience to him.
  * Les offrandes que Dieu a ordonné aux  Israélites de donner étaient une démonstration de leur dévotion et obéissance envers Lui.
  * The names of the different offerings, such as "burnt offering" and "peace offering," indicated what kind of offering was being given.
  * Les noms des différentes offrandes, telles que "l'holocauste" et "le sacrifice de paix" indiquaient le genre d'offrande. 
  * Sacrifices to God often involved the killing of an animal.
  * On tuait souvent un animal pour les sacrifices envers Dieu 
  * Only the sacrifice of Jesus, God's perfect, sinless Son, can completely cleanse people from sin. Animal sacrifices could never do that.
  * Seulement le sacrifice de Jésus, le Fils sans péché et parfait de Dieu est capable de purifier les gens de leur péché. Aucun des sacrifices d'animaux  ne pouvait l'accomplir. 
   The figurative expression "offer yourselves as a living sacrifice" means, "live your life in complete obedience to God, giving up everything in order to serve him."
   L'expression figurative "s'offrir en sacrifice vivant" veut dire "consacrer sa vie à vivre dans l'obéissance totale envers Dieu, à renoncer à toutes choses pour le servir.

===== Translation Suggestions =====
  * The term "offering" could also be translated as "a gift to God" or "something given to God" or "something valuable that is presented to God."
  * Depending on the context, the term "sacrifice" could also be translated as "something valuable given in worship" or "a special animal killed and presented to God."
  * The action, "to sacrifice" could be translated as, "to give up something valuable" or "to kill an animal and give it to God."
  * Another way to translate "present yourself as a living sacrifice" could be, "As you live your life, offer yourself to God as completely as an animal that is offered on an altar."

Une fois que Noé fut sorti du bateau, Il bâtit un autel et sacrifia quelques animaux de chaque espèce qui étaient destinés au **sacrifice**. Dieu fut content du **sacrifice** et bénit Noé et sa famille.
  * **[[:fr:obs:notes:frames:05-06|[05-06]]]**"Take Isaac, your only son, and kill him as a **sacrifice**  to me." Again Abraham obeyed God and prepared to **sacrifice**  his son. “Prends Isaac, ton fils unique, et **sacrifie**-le pour Moi.” Abraham obéit encore à Dieu et se prépara à **sacrifier** son fils.
  * **[[:fr:obs:notes:frames:05-09|[05-09]]]** God had provided the ram to be the **sacrifice**  instead of Isaac. Dieu donna un bélier à offrir en **sacrifice** à la place d'Isaac.
  * **[[:fr:obs:notes:frames:13-09|[13-09]]]** Anyone who disobeyed God's law could bring an animal to the Tent of Meeting as a **sacrifice**  to God. A priest would kill the animal and burn it on the altar. The blood of the animal that was **sacrificed**  covered the person's sin and made that person clean in God's sight. Si une personne avait désobéi à une des lois de Dieu, elle pouvait apporter un animal à l'autel devant la Tente en **sacrifice** à Dieu. Un prêtre tuerait l'animal et le brulerait sur l'autel.   * **[[:fr:obs:notes:frames:48-06|[48-06]]]** Jesus is the Great High Priest. Unlike other priests, he offered himself as the only **sacrifice**  that could to take away the sin of all the people in the world.
  * **[[:fr:obs:notes:frames:48-08|[48-08]]]** But God provided Jesus, the Lamb of God, as a **sacrifice**  to die in our place.
  * **[[:fr:obs:notes:frames:49-11|[49-11]]]** Because Jesus **sacrificed**  himself, God can forgive any sin, even terrible sins.
