====== captive, captivity/captif(ve),captivité ======

====== les Faits: ======  

===== Definition: Définition: =====

The terms "captive" and "captivity" refer to capturing someone and holding him as a prisoner against his will. Usually this is in the context of an army taking their enemies captive.

Les termes "captif" et "captivité" signifient capturer quelqu'un et le détenir malgré lui comme prisonier. C'est normalement dans le contexte d'une armée qui capture ses ennemis en tant que prisoniers.

  * The Israelites from the kingdom of Judah were taken into captivity by the Babylonian army and kept captive in the kingdom of Babylonia for 70 years.
  * Les Israélites du royaume de Juda ont été emmenés en captivité par l'armée Babylonienne et ont été prisoniers dans le royaume de Babylonie pendant 70 ans.
  * God often allowed Israel to be held captive by an enemy as punishment for their rebellion against him.
  * Dieu a souvent permis qu'Israël soit détenu en captivité par leur ennemi à cause de leur rébellion contre Lui.
  * The idiom "be taken captive" or "to take captive" is another way of talking about capturing someone.
  * L'idiome "être pris captif" ou "prendre captif" est une autre façon de parler de capturer quelqu'un.
  * In a figurative sense, the apostle Paul tells Christians to "take captive" every thought and make it obedient to Christ.
  * Au sens figuré, l'apôtre Paul parle "d'amener toute pensée captive à l'obéissance de Christ. "
  * He also talks about how a person can be "taken captive" by sin, which means he is "controlled by" sin.
  * Il parle aussi d'une personne qui peut être "tenue en captivité" par le péché, c'est à dire "sous l'emprise" du péché.

====== Translation Suggestions ======

  * Depending on the context, to be "held captive" could also be translated by, "not allowed to be free" or "kept in prison" or "forced to live in a foreign country."
  * The term, "taken captive" could be translated as, "captured" or "imprisoned" or "forced to go to a foreign land."
  * The term "captives" could also be translated as, "captured people" or "enslaved people."
  * Depending on the context, "captivity" could also be translated as, "imprisonment" or "exile" or "forced stay in a foreign country."
